The Hook - What is Cavebreakers?

[Placeholder: Striking concept art - dark cave entrance lit by torchlight, silhouettes visible]

Elevator Pitch: A co-op survival horror game where 1-4 Stone Age scavengers must outwit terrifying mythical creatures and each other to escape a cursed wilderness.

Core Concept: Balance desperate teamwork with treacherous sabotage in a tense, atmospheric prehistoric world.

Genre: Multiplayer Co-op Survival Horror with Social Deduction Elements.

Quick Comps: Think Dead by Daylight meets The Forest with the co-op tension of Left 4 Dead and the potential betrayal of Among Us.

The Core Gameplay Loop

[Placeholder: Stylized flowchart graphic with cave painting / stone texture icons]
  • SEARCH: Spawn into a procedurally generated wilderness fraught with danger.
  • SCAVENGE: Gather essential resources (flint, wood, herbs, bones).
  • CRAFT: Create crude tools, torches, weapons, and cunning traps.
  • SECURE: Light vital Bonfires for temporary safety, perks, and minor healing.
  • SURVIVE: Evade or confront deadly mythical creatures using stealth, environment, and wits.
  • ESCAPE: Complete primal objectives before the darkness consumes you.
  • UPGRADE: Spend salvaged Artifacts on permanent perks for subsequent hunts.

Key Pillars: Exploration, Resource Management, Strategic Evasion/Combat, Dynamic Teamwork vs. Sabotage, Meta-Progression.

Meet the Horrors

[Placeholder: Gritty concept art/renders collage - Wendigo, Werebear, Werewolf in atmospheric settings]

Headline: Outwit, Don't Just Out-Damage.

  • Wendigo: Ethereal mimic, fears fire, nightmarishly fast & stealthy. Exploit its fear & gaze aversion. (Weak Spot: Head)
  • Werebear: Hulking brute, charges, tracks by scent, demolishes weak structures. Lure into large traps. (Vulnerable post-rage)
  • Werewolf: Agile pack hunter, targets the isolated, reacts to loud noises & multiple lights. Use coordination & distraction.

Emphasis: Each beast demands unique strategies. Learn their behaviors, exploit weaknesses, and use the environment to survive.

The Twist: Sabotage & Social Tension

Headline: Trust is a Resource. Spend it Wisely.

You and a teammate are fleeing a enraged Werebear. You round a corner, finding a hidden spike pit trap you crafted earlier. Your teammate is right behind you... The Werebear is closing in... What do you do?

Outcome: Betrayal!

You deftly sidestep the trap. Your teammate, caught unaware, tumbles in just as the Werebear catches up. Their screams are drowned out by the beast's roar.

You escape, but the guilt (and maybe 'Bad Karma') follows... You keep the resources you both gathered.

Outcome: Desperate Teamwork!

You yell a warning, grabbing your teammate and pulling them aside just as they nearly trigger the trap. The Werebear barrels past, momentarily confused.

You both scramble away, escaping together. You gain a 'Survivor Bonus' if the whole team makes it out, strengthening future runs.

(Sabotage includes resource hoarding, noise misdirection, baiting creatures, and more...)

The Hunter

Strength: Higher base damage, better tracking. Weakness: Noisier movement, larger profile. Ability: Primal Scream (Short-range creature scare).
[Icon: Footprint/Eye]

The Scout

Strength: Faster movement, quieter steps, better stamina. Weakness: Lower health, less combat proficient. Ability: Pathfinder (Briefly highlights nearby resources/threats).
[Icon: Bag/Tools]

The Scavenger

Strength: Finds more resources, crafts faster, larger inventory. Weakness: Slower base speed, average combat. Ability: Resourceful (Chance to not consume item on use).
[Icon: Staff/Bonfire]

The Elder

Strength: Buffs nearby allies (e.g., faster healing), resists fear effects better. Weakness: Frail, slowest movement. Ability: Soothing Chant (Reduces fear/panic in an area).
[Icon: Skull/Herbs]

The Shaman

Strength: Better healing/herbalism, can interact with ritual sites faster. Weakness: Vulnerable while chanting/healing. Ability: Ward (Places temporary small ward repelling minor creatures).

Art Style & Immersion

[Placeholder: Atmospheric collage - first-person view hiding in ferns, bonfire lighting a clearing, creepy cave interior, minimalist UI mockup]
  • Style: Grounded, Gritty Stone Age Horror with Realistic Textures.
  • Perspective: Immersive First-Person to maximize tension.
  • Goal: Striking visuals via atmosphere, lighting (dynamic torchlight, eerie fog), and detailed creature design without over-scoped assets.
  • Influences: *The Forest* (Atmosphere), *Far Cry Primal* (Aesthetic), *Hunt: Showdown* (Tension & Grit).
  • UI: Minimalist & diegetic where possible (e.g., heartbeat sounds for health), avoiding screen clutter.

Sound Design: The Unseen Threat

[Placeholder: Abstract visual - sound waves emanating from darkness, or a player reacting to an off-screen noise]

Headline: Hear Them Before You See Them.

  • Dynamic Music: Primal drums, ethereal whispers, tension tracks that react to gameplay.
  • Directional & Occluded Audio: Pinpoint creature locations through footsteps, growls, *chilling mimicry*.
  • Atmospheric Ambience: Crackling fires, wind through trees, dripping caves, unsettling distant calls.
  • Player Feedback: Heavy breathing, pained grunts, footsteps varying by surface – all conveying state and presence.

Impact: Sound is paramount for immersion, warning, jump scares, and locating threats (and maybe allies...).

Game Modes & Replayability

[Placeholder: Simple cave painting style icons for each mode]

Core Modes

  • Scavenge (1-4p): Standard co-op, escape together... or don't. Sabotage possible.
  • Sabotage (2-4p): Last survivor standing takes the glory (and unique loot!). High betrayal focus.
  • Solo (1p): You against the wilderness. Heightened tension, tweaked balance.
  • Friendly (1-4p): Pure co-op fun. No sabotage, no friendly fire.

Replayability

  • Procedural Map Generation
  • Deep Perk System & Builds
  • Emergent Player Stories
  • Multiple Creatures & Modes
  • Future Content Updates
